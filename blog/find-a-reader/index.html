Find a Reader

Aug 14th, 2014

This blog is ‘sequential’. Please start at the beginning and work forward from there with the bottom-right-hand arrows.

Now that you have started your book with some writings in ‘{Title}_1.doc’, your next goal is to find a reader. To this reader you will say

This is a start of my book {Title}. I would like you to read it and give me your personal feedback and insights. What do you like and dislike about the writing? Are there any words or phrases that you don’t understand? What do you think of the tone? What do you think of the title?

Don’t worry about ‘editing’ the writing, I will pay for a commercial editor. But feel free to do so if you need to as part of reading the book

You are not asking the person to read the finished product, or to read anything more than what you wrote so far. You are asking them to read.

Lots of people like to read. My daughters love to read, and my youngest was my first reader. When she started reading and smiling at my writing, I was an ‘author’ (with a lowercase ‘a’). That is all you are striving for. To become the smallest of ‘authors’, so you can grow and flourish into being an ‘AUTHOR’ one capital at a time :–)

So go find a reader, you budding ‘author’… I will wait.

Oh, and if you are curious what my first reader read first, I believe it was the samples here: http://slumsofpaloalto.org/samples/. Possibly in a different order.
