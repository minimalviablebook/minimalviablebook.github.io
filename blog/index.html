Find a Reader

Aug 14th, 2014

This blog is ‘sequential’. Please start at the beginning and work forward from there with the bottom-right-hand arrows.

Now that you have started your book with some writings in ‘{Title}_1.doc’, your next goal is to find a reader. To this reader you will say

This is a start of my book {Title}. I would like you to read it and give me your personal feedback and insights. What do you like and dislike about the writing? Are there any words or phrases that you don’t understand? What do you think of the tone? What do you think of the title?

Don’t worry about ‘editing’ the writing, I will pay for a commercial editor. But feel free to do so if you need to as part of reading the book

You are not asking the person to read the finished product, or to read anything more than what you wrote so far. You are asking them to read.

Lots of people like to read. I am sure you can find someone to hand a printout too. And do give them paper. Yes, reading electronically is easy, but commenting electronically is not easy. You could even choose some neat paper color to print your writing on, so it doesn’t get lost on a desk.

My daughters love to read, and my youngest was my first reader. When she started reading and smiling at my writing, I was an ‘author’ (with a lowercase ‘a’). That is all you are striving for. To become the smallest of ‘authors’, so you can grow and flourish into being an ‘AUTHOR’ one capital at a time :–)

Oh, and if you are curious what my first reader read first, I believe it was the samples here: http://slumsofpaloalto.com/samples/. Possibly in a different order.

So go find a reader, you budding ‘author’… I will wait.

Start by Writing

Aug 13th, 2014

This may seem to be a no-brainer, but you start a book by writing. It doesn’t have to be much of anything. It could be complete crap I encourage you to write complete crap if that is all you can do. Because writing anything is the start. Everything until that point is just dreaming. You want to be an auther right? So go write! One paragraph. “It was a dark and stormy night…” or whatever. I will wait.

(Next day)

OK. Did you write something? Yes! Awesome!

If not, let us try this again. The day I became an author was a Monday in May. I had been biking around Google for many months (even years) and while biking all those months/years I had been telling myself stories. I would write stories in my head and listen to them in my head.

But this particular Monday when I got home, I did something ‘new’. I wrote down the story that I just had in my head. I created a word document:

  • SlumsOfPA_mlf1a1.doc

The first part is the title of the book: “The Slums of Palo Alto”. The second part after the underscore are my initials and then a version counter ‘1a1’. Together these form part of a vector-clock, but that is a different topic. If you ignore the ‘mlf1a’ we get a file named:

  • SlumsOfPA_1.doc

OK. Now you go and create a file named ‘{Title}_1.doc’ where you replace the ‘{Title}’ part with your actual title. I will wait.

Note that some day in the future you can change your title. So don’t worry about the title being “final”, it is just a title.

Now into this file type the title in full as the very first line. For me it is:

The Slums of Palo Alto

Decide if you need a subtitle, and if you have one (right this moment, doesn’t have to be fine) write it:

How to be ecstatically happy after ten failed startups

Hit a couple ‘enters’ to get some space.

And now the most important part. Just write! It doesn’t have to be the start, it should just be a paragraph that could possibly be in your book, somewhere.

The putting green on the balcony overlooked Santa Monica pier. We were in the suite previously owned by Lawrence Welk, and could watch the taping of Bay Watch every Monday. During the day it was incredibly beautifulâ€¦ it was the first of a series of too beautiful to believe buildings that would affect my life. At night it was a bit scary. We were building a news palace for a Saudi Prince.

Now you do the same. I will wait.

Site Launch!

Aug 12th, 2014

I have been writing a book http://slumsofpaloalto.com/. How I started, progressed, and finished is very different from how others think about books and publishing. It is insanely different. And insanely better. I hope describing it helps you.

I am mostly going to walk through chronologically because I think I generally did things in the right order. So each blog posting will be a replay of time.

An important thing to know about me is that I am a “startup specialist”. I launch companies and try to get them to a point where other people want to buy them or they can “go public” on stock market. This is the angle I am coming from. I am certainly a capable writer, but that is mostly technically oriented. And the book I wrote is an “augmented memoir”, which is a significantly easier thing to write than a novel or really any other form of book.

I can not help you be a great writer. I may be a horrible writer. But in a ridiculously short period of time, other people were reading my book. An actual book. Not a manuscript, draft, etc. It was a book.

That ridiculously short period of time from having never written a book to having a book that others are reading? One month! By two months I was at 150 pages and had mailed my book to Stephen Colbert, John Stewart, and Sheryl Sandberg. Plus dozens of people that I could easily reach in my home town. And the time-lines continue with that insane speed. Oh, and I was rejected by 42 literary agents in that same period. More than a dozen by the end of one month. So the time-lines and effectiveness are insanely different.

When normal is not giving you what you want, insanity (“think different”) can be insanely better!
