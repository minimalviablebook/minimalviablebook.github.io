Start by Writing

Aug 13th, 2014

This may seem to be a no-brainer, but you start a book by writing. It doesn’t have to be much of anything. It could be complete crap I encourage you to write complete crap if that is all you can do. Because writing anything is the start. Everything until that point is just dreaming. You want to be an auther right? So go write! One paragraph. “It was a dark and stormy night…” or whatever. I will wait.

(Next day)

OK. Did you write something? Yes! Awesome!

If not, let us try this again. The day I became an author was a Monday in May. I had been biking around Google for many months (even years) and while biking all those months/years I had been telling myself stories. I would write stories in my head and listen to them in my head.

But this particular Monday when I got home, I did something ‘new’. I wrote down the story that I just had in my head. I created a word document:

  • SlumsOfPA_mlf1a1.doc

The first part is the title of the book: “The Slums of Palo Alto”. The second part after the underscore are my initials and then a version counter ‘1a1’. Together these form part of a vector-clock, but that is a different topic. If you ignore the ‘mlf1a’ we get a file named:

  • SlumsOfPA_1.doc

OK. Now you go and create a file named ‘{Title}_1.doc’ where you replace the ‘{Title}’ part with your actual title. I will wait.

Note that some day in the future you can change your title. So don’t worry about the title being “final”, it is just a title.

Now into this file type the title in full as the very first line. For me it is:

The Slums of Palo Alto

Decide if you need a subtitle, and if you have one (right this moment, doesn’t have to be fine) write it:

How to be ecstatically happy after ten failed startups

Hit a couple ‘enters’ to get some space.

And now the most important part. Just write! It doesn’t have to be the start, it should just be a paragraph that could possibly be in your book, somewhere.

The putting green on the balcony overlooked Santa Monica pier. We were in the suite previously owned by Lawrence Welk, and could watch the taping of Bay Watch every Monday. We were building a news palace for a Saudi Prince.

Now you do the same. I will wait.
